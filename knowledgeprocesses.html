Knowledge Processes
KM is not about owning knowledge — it's about streamlining the processes that involve it. These three processes are central to effective KM.
01
Acquisition
Obtaining knowledge. Training, learning, researching, analyzing, doing.

02
Capture
Generating new knowledge and documenting it.

03
Integration
Sharing org-wide. Enabling duplication and driving efficiency and new knowledge.
1
Acquisition
The act of obtaining knowledge through experience — training, learning, researching, analyzing, or simply doing the work.
2
Capture
Generating new knowledge and documenting it so it can outlive the individual who created it.
3
Integration
Sharing knowledge org-wide. Allowing duplication so it drives both efficiency and the creation of new knowledge.
◆
KM Manager's Role
Improve the efficiency of these three processes by introducing better tools and techniques to gather and share knowledge.
