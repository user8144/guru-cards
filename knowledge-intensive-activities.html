Knowledge-Intensive Activities
Common activities that drive productivity, creativity, and efficiency. KM focuses on these because they're where knowledge has the highest ROI.
1
After-Action Reviews (AARs)
▼
1. What was supposed to happen?
2. What actually happened?
3. Why was there a difference?
4. What can we learn from this?
Must be done regularly and soon after the initiative.
Results should be shared and applied to the next initiative.
2
Project Management
▼
KMI
Build on GANTT charts with a Before / During / After approach:
Before: Share knowledge so the whole team starts at the highest level possible.
During: Day-of action reviews. Learn and improve in real time.
After: Retrospective. What nuggets should pass to the next team?
3
Process Management
▼
KMI
"Connect & Collect" — prioritize relationships first, then documentation.
Build relationships and learn from people for reuse.
Checklists are game changing.
4
Improving Meetings
▼
Start on time.
Have an agenda.
Only relevant people required.
Record decisions.
