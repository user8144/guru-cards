Makes life easier. Reduces inefficiencies, frees up time, lets them focus on their core role.
Change management
Deliberate process of building org readiness, awareness, and cultural buy-in through small quick wins before scaling — ensuring people are prepared to adopt new ways of working before significant resources are committed.
Build readiness first Quick wins = momentum Cultural buy-in Then scale

Maturity models
Ways to measure org growth and transformation — ensuring ongoing improvement. KM is an ongoing, maturity-driven process.
1
Initial / Ad hoc
No formal KM. Knowledge siloed.
2
Developing
Some processes. Inconsistent adoption.
3
Defined
Documented KM strategy. Org-wide awareness.
4
Managed
Measured KM outcomes. Continuous improvement.
5
Optimizing
KM embedded in culture. Strategic competitive advantage.
KM roles — from worker to CKO
Chief Knowledge Officer CKO
Creates KM vision, secures exec buy-in, champions budget and culture
KM Manager CKM
Transitions KM from tactical startup to funded strategic program
Knowledge Specialist CKS
Deep SME brought in once a KM Bull is selected
KM Practitioner CKP
Runs day-to-day KM activities, drives grassroots startup
KM Instructor
Designs and delivers the change management and training plan
Knowledge Worker
Everyone. Creates, shares, or applies knowledge through daily work
Bulls vs squirrels
Bull
Enterprise-wide
Large initiative requiring time, resources, and funding. High impact, high change across the org.
Squirrel
Quick win
Builds momentum and demonstrates KM value. Essential before scaling to bigger initiatives.
